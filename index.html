▸ INFLATION — THEORY PRIMER

Cosmic Inflation

Inflation is a period of accelerated expansion in the very early universe (t~10⁻³⁶ s), driven by the potential energy V(φ) of a scalar field φ (the inflaton). It solves the horizon, flatness, and monopole problems and generates the primordial density perturbations observed in the CMB.

Slow-Roll Parameters

ε(φ) = ½(V′/V)² — measures the slope of V. Inflation occurs while ε < 1 and ends when ε = 1.

η(φ) = V″/V — measures the curvature of V. The slow-roll approximation requires ε, |η| ≪ 1.

CMB Observables

nₛ = 1 − 6ε★ + 2η★ is the scalar spectral index (tilt of the power spectrum). Planck 2018: nₛ = 0.965 ± 0.004.

r = 16ε★ is the tensor-to-scalar ratio (gravitational waves). Current bound: r < 0.064 (Planck+BICEP/Keck).

E-folds & Pivot Scale

The number of e-folds is N(φ) = ∫ V/V′ dφ (in reduced Planck units). CMB observables are evaluated at the pivot field value φ★ corresponding to N★ ≈ 50–60 e-folds before inflation ends, depending on the reheating history.

Mukhanov–Sasaki Equation

The exact quantum mode equation for scalar perturbations is u_k″ + (k² − z″/z) u_k = 0, where z = aφ̇/H is the pump field and primes denote conformal-time derivatives.

Starting in the Bunch-Davies vacuum u_k = e⁻ⁱᵏᵗ/√(2k) deep inside the horizon, each mode oscillates then freezes out after horizon crossing (k = aH), seeding the classical perturbation ℛ_k = u_k/z.

MS vs Slow-Roll Accuracy

The slow-roll result 𝒫ℛ = H²/(8π²ε) evaluated at horizon exit is accurate to ~1% for standard single-field models with ε, |η| ≪ 1. Deviations appear near features in V(φ) (e.g. bumps, steps) or when slow-roll is violated — exactly the physics the Relative Error and Running nₛ(k) plots diagnose. The tensor spectrum 𝒫T = 8k³/(2π²) × |v_k|²/a² is solved analogously, with a″/a = a²H²(2−ε) replacing z″/z.
